Q7.
Ans-The size of a C structure is the sum of the sizes of its individual members, plus any padding added by the 
compiler to ensure proper alignment.

In C, the size of a structure is determined by the following factors:

1.The size of each member of the structure.
2.The alignment requirements of each member of the structure.
3.Any padding added by the compiler to ensure that each member is properly aligned.
